🔹 Step 3: Add favicon link in <head>
Inside your index.html (or in Next.js → app/layout.tsx or pages/_document.tsx), add:

html
Copy
Edit
<!-- Favicon for most browsers -->
<link rel="icon" href="/favicon.ico" sizes="any" />

<!-- PNG versions -->
<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="32x32" href="/favicon-32x32.png" />
<link rel="icon" type="image/png" sizes="16x16" href="/favicon-16x16.png" />

<!-- Apple iOS -->
<link rel="apple-touch-icon" href="/apple-touch-icon.png" />

<!-- Optional: Safari pinned tab -->
<link rel="mask-icon" href="/safari-pinned-tab.svg" color="#5bbad5" />
🔹 Step 4: Clear cache & test
Browsers cache favicons aggressively.

After adding, hard refresh (Ctrl + Shift + R on Windows, Cmd + Shift + R on Mac).

You may also need to clear browser cache or try in incognito mode.

✅ Now your favicon should appear in the browser tab and when someone saves your site to their phone home screen.
